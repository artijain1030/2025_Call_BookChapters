About the Handbook

This book presents a comprehensive exploration of next-generation AI architectures for climate resilience, focusing on robust hazard prediction and efficient mitigation through advanced data-driven approaches. It details the use of large-scale, heterogeneous environmental datasets—including satellite imagery, GIS, hydrological and meteorological time- series, and real-time sensor data—alongside state-of-the-art preprocessing, feature engineering, and data fusion techniques.

The technical core covers cutting-edge machine learning frameworks such as deep convolutional neural networks for spatial analysis, recurrent networks and transformers for temporal modeling, and ensemble learning for improved prediction reliability. Special emphasis is placed on spatiotemporal modeling, attention mechanisms for interpretability, and hybrid architectures that integrate physical process models with data-driven AI. The integration of AI with cloud platforms like Google Earth Engine enables scalable, near real-time hazard monitoring and automated early warning systems.

The book highlights explainable AI (XAI) methods—such as SHAP and Grad-CAM—for transparent decision support in disaster management. Case studies showcase AI applications in wildfire detection, flood forecasting, drought monitoring, and landslide risk assessment, with validation against simulation models and ground truth. It further examines AI-enabled sensor networks, edge computing, and emerging technologies like generative AI for synthetic data, digital twins for environment simulation, and quantum computing for accelerating climate models, serving as a vital resource for researchers and practitioners advancing AI-based climate hazard solutions.

This upcoming book is being submitted to Elsevier for publication and Scopus indexing, ensuring high academic visibility and global reach.

Potential Chapter Titles

Part I: Foundations and Systems Framework

Chapter 1: Climate Hazards and Decision Contexts

Establishes hazard typology, multi-hazard risk cascades, stakeholder decision processes under deep uncertainty, and advanced cost-loss optimization with decision-theoretic frameworks for strategy calibration.

Chapter 2: Environmental Data Ecosystems and Assimilation

Satellite/RS, GIS, hydro-met networks, IoT sensors, and crowd data integration via advanced data assimilation (EnKF, 4D-Var) coupled with physics models. QA/QC at scale, federated learning for privacy-preserving fusion, and benchmark datasets.

Chapter 3: Advanced Spatiotemporal AI/ML Foundations

Domain-specialized architectures (Graph Transformers, Neural Operators) beyond standard CNN/RNN; PINNs and Fourier Neural Operators for conservation-law compliance; rigorous sharp-functionals metrics and conformal prediction for rare-event reliability.

Chapter 4: AI Failure Modes and Robustness in Climate Hazard Systems

Adversarial robustness, temporal distribution shifts, epistemic uncertainty propagation, and regulatory-compliant risk assessment frameworks.

Part II: Hazard-Specific Advanced Prediction Systems

Chapter 5: Geospatial Foundation Models for Remote Sensing

Self-supervised vision transformers and diffusion models for zero-shot hazard detection/segmentation, coupled with SAR/optical fusion for all-weather monitoring.

Chapter 6: Physics-Guided Time-Series Forecasting

Neural operators + 4D-Var assimilation for subseasonal extremes; regime-aware Transformers with climate oscillator conditioning.

Chapter 7: Flood Forecasting and Basin Modeling

Coupled hydrologic-AI approaches integrating atmospheric data, rainfall-runoff models, and hydrodynamic simulations. Inundation mapping and extent prediction. Validation against flood observation networks and historical records.

Chapter 8: Drought Monitoring and Early Warning

Integration of climate, hydrological, and environmental indicators with AI analytics for onset and severity detection. Real-time decision support enabling timely mitigation actions.

Chapter 9: Wildfire Detection and Spread Prediction

Satellite-based fire detection, fire spread modeling coupled with fire simulators, and impact-based forecasting. Real-world case studies demonstrating operational deployment.

Chapter 10: Landslide Susceptibility and Triggering Prediction

Ensemble AI/ML models with rainfall-threshold analysis and slope stability models. High-risk zone identification and early warning system architecture.

Chapter 11: Cyclones, Storms, and Extreme Events

Real-time storm tracking, intensity prediction, and coupling with numerical weather prediction (NWP) models. Impact-based early warnings; recent AI advances in tracking accuracy and lead time.

Part III: Operations, Validations, and Reliability

Chapter 12: Uncertainty Quantification and Reliability

Calibration assessment, ensemble methods for robustness, and explainability techniques (SHAP, Grad-CAM, attention mechanisms). Validation against regulatory and observational standards, including benchmark intercomparison.

Chapter 13: Real-Time Decision Support and Scalable Early Warning Architectures

Hierarchical Partially Observable Markov Decision Process (POMDPs) for adaptive alerting; digital twin pipelines for scenario testing and human-in-loop optimization.

Chapter 14: Cloud, Edge, and Digital Twins for Environmental AI

Google Earth Engine, on-device edge computing, and digital twin frameworks for dynamic environment simulation and energy-efficient analysis. Resilience support, sustainable resource allocation in uncertain conditions.

Chapter 15: MLOps for Climate Systems

ML lifecycle management: data versioning, drift detection, continuous monitoring, A/B testing frameworks, reproducibility, and regulatory audit trails for climate-critical deployments.

Chapter 16: Logistics and Humanitarian Operations

Supply chain optimization for disaster response, pre-positioning strategies, and multi-agent RL for dynamic routing in disrupted networks. Real-world case studies from humanitarian organizations.

Part IV: Systems, Governance, and Responsible Implementation

Chapter 17: Governance, Ethics, and Data Policy

Governance frameworks, ethical AI deployment, and data policies for climate systems. Emphasis on Explainable AI (XAI)- SHAP and Grad-CAM for trust, privacy protection, and equitable outcomes. Discussion of AI misuse risks and safeguards in high-stakes decision contexts.

Chapter 18: Regional and Community Implementation

AI-enabled climate resilience in developing countries and vulnerable regions. Regional collaboration, cross-border early warning systems, and citizen science integration. Equity-centered adaptation strategies.

Chapter 19: Frontier Technologies and Future Directions

Exploration of Generative AI for synthetic scenario generation, Quantum Computing applications in large-scale climate modeling, and emerging paradigms in environmental AI.
